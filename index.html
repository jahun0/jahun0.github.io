About

I am a captain in the Republic of Korea Army and an assistant professor at the Korea Army Academy. My research focuses on multi-agent systems decision-makingâ€”how teams of autonomous agents learn to coordinate under uncertainty, partial observability, and real-world constraints. I am especially interested in bridging algorithmic advances with deployment-relevant evaluation, building end-to-end systems that remain reliable under distribution shift and limited resources.

My recent work includes multi-robot reinforcement learning for coordinated behaviors (including drone-soccer settings) and temporal graph methods to detect coordinated patterns in online influence activity, with the long-term goal of enabling early-warning decision support. I hold an M.S. in Computer Engineering from Texas A&M University and a B.S. in Military Science and Information Science from the Korea Military Academy. I am also supported by a ROKA/MoD PhD scholarship for overseas doctoral study.

Research Interests

  • Multi-agent reinforcement learning for coordination, strategy, and team decision-making under uncertainty
  • Distributed autonomy: scalable learning and control for multi-robot teams under real-world constraints
  • Graph-based learning for coordinated behavior detection and influence-operation pattern discovery
  • Temporal modeling for early-warning signals and lead-time-aware detection in dynamic systems
  • Evaluation-driven AI systems: robustness, reliability, and deployment-aware benchmarking
